{# ─────────── Interpretation ─────────── #}
Interpretation:
  • The lipid profile is the primary screen for cardiovascular risk and dyslipidaemia. A 12-hour fast is recommended for accurate triglyceride measurement.
  • LDL cholesterol is the principal target of lipid-lowering therapy; lower is better in patients with established atherosclerotic disease, diabetes, or multiple risk factors.
  • HDL cholesterol is inversely related to coronary risk. Levels < 40 mg/dL (men) or < 50 mg/dL (women) are considered low.
  • Elevated triglycerides are commonly seen in metabolic syndrome, diabetes, obesity, alcoholism, hypothyroidism and nephrotic syndrome. Levels ≥ 500 mg/dL increase the risk of acute pancreatitis.
  • Non-HDL cholesterol (Total – HDL) captures all atherogenic particles (LDL + VLDL + remnants) and is a better predictor of cardiovascular events in patients with high triglycerides than LDL alone.
  • TC/HDL and LDL/HDL ratios integrate the protective and atherogenic fractions; ratios above the "ideal" thresholds indicate elevated risk.
  • Persistent dyslipidaemia warrants lifestyle modification — diet, exercise, weight reduction, smoking cessation — and statin therapy where indicated by overall cardiovascular risk.
